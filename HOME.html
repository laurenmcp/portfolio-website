PIECES OF MY JOURNAL

If I could be your neighbor

I’m standing at my bedroom door
looking down the hall toward the room
you moved out of twelve years ago.
Your graduation portraits linger
on the wall. It’s as if I’m looking
at myself with different eyes. I
see myself twelve years in
the future where you are today.
Across the continent, in the land
of serial killers (which I hope
you still love) and redwood forests,
with a friendly husband and rescued
cats. In October, I petted Takk
for the last time, accepting licks
as a goodbye present. You showed me
your garden, which you and Travis
planned with geeky precision. I laughed
when I saw the bougainvillea-
a sad, wooden vine, propped against
a trellis, propped against the settling
house. When I left, a string popped
in my chest from the violin we shared.
Our orchestra teacher retired last year.
...
A week ago you sent a photo
of the bougainvillea with a thickened
trunk, branches reaching out. I read
online that she has wooden spikes
that easily pierce through skin.
The bougainvillea is more like
a fence than a flower.
...
I have made a cappuccino
with cayenne pepper, as you taught me.
I still use oat milk, though you now
prefer cow’s. I remember your
silly sounds. Like a duck on the radio,
you let out garbled quacks,
urging me to move whenever
I was in your way in the kitchen.
...
My assortment of cacti and
succulents from the grocery store
no longer satisfy me, though they
grow rather well in my east-facing
window. I considered growing
a bougainvillea, but I don’t
think it would thrive in this climate.
If I lived next door to you in twelve
years, our bougainvilleas could grow
together, stretching across the fence
and linking spiky arms.
